Shelter to Soldier

Shelter to Soldier is a non-profit located in Oceanside, California. Their moto:” SAVING LIVES, TWO AT A TIME™” is the perfect summary of what they do as they bring post-9/11 combat veterans together with trained rescue dogs and as in their words they “help both recover and move forward.” We chose this non-profit because we loved how they were helping not only the animals but also our veterans at the same time.

The Problem

Veterans with accessibility challenges due to PTSD, and traumatic brain injury need a clear application process when they are applying for a therapy dog.

Our Hypothesis

We believe that by streamlining the application process, veterans with accessibility challenges will achieve greater application success while reducing abandoned applications. In the future Shelter to Soldier could track their abandoned application rate to see if our changes made a difference.

• Ability to create and account and save progress
• Ability to clearly see process and what documentation is required
• Decrease use of large text boxes and instead create information chunking to decrease errors.

User Definition

We defined two separate user personas: one being the donor and one being the veteran applicant. Knowing that the Veteran might have potentially significant challenges due to their injuries we hypothesized they might need a clear and straight forward application process.

Ideation

Our color scheme is similar to the current one but we added a bit more brightness to make it more exciting. We also took the logo and restructured the text so it could fit in a smaller space.
